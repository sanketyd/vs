Everyone possesses a huge potential to excel. Now it's time to gear up and channelize this potential in a constructive way. The Essay Writing Competition is mainly focused on enabling you to explore the "real" secrets of Life. Just pen down your ideas coupled with that of Swami Vivekananda's.
Note: Essays must be original. Essays with "Copy-Paste" content will be disqualified.

Submission mail-id: vsamiti@iitk.ac.in
Date & Time: 11:59 pm, 22nd January, 2012 (Sunday).
Registration link: Go to top


Submission Guidelines: All participants must register themselves at the above link. Please mention all the same details in your article. Essays may be hand-written or emailed to vsamiti@iitk.ac.in before 11:59 pm Sunday, 22nd January, 2012. Hand written essays may be handed to the essay competition coordinator.

Judgment criteria: Essays will be judged on three criteria:

  1. Presentation: This includes mainly the style of writing and its capacity to hold the reader's interest, and the logical connection of ideas.

  3. Content & Analysis: This takes into account the breadth and depth of research and writing and the completeness of the logical analysis.

Extempore competition

This is all about clarity of thought and ability to express your views. How convincingly you can state your views? Extempore demands good communication skills, social awareness and ability to present your points on the platform.

Participants will be required to select a random topic from a basket of several relevant topics of interest. If he/she is not comfortable with the topic, he/she can make one more attempt of choosing a topic. Once a topic is chosen, 5 minutes time may be given to plan the content. The participants will need to speak 2-3 minutes and answer at most 2 judge/audience questions.

The participants will be evaluated on the basis of talk content, awareness about the topic and communication skills like language fluency, confidence level and body language. Few sample topics include Impact of Corruption on Economics, Responsibility of Media, Population of India - an asset or curse, Concerns of Indian society, Misery of Indian Farmers, Social Networking misleads Youth, etc.

Date & Time: 15:00 - 16:15, 15th January, 2012 (Sunday).
Venue: L-16, Lecture Hall Complex.
Registration link: Go to top

Prizes for Extempore competition: Best 10 people will be awarded with prize and certificate.

Two prizes for best audience evaluation: Audience will be required to evaluate the extempore on the scale of 10 for each participant. The two most consistent evaluations will be awarded with a prize.
