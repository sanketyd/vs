Specific vision for the present year:

Swami Vivekananda's 150th Birthday commences on January 12, 2013. The 150th Birth Anniversary of Swami Vivekananda is a great occasion for spreading his inspirational teachings among the youth of India. We are celebrating this august occasion with a 4 yrs. plan of series of events during 2011 to 2014. Our nation requires people with high values and strong determination who will serve the nation and solve its numerous challenging problems. The bright and talented students of this premiere institute have immense potential which must be channelized for the betterment of the human society. We are celebrating Vivekananda Youth Convention 2012 followed by medical health camp, invited talks, short socio-spiritual tours, personality development workshop and other community service etc. Our goal is to motivate the youth towards a positive way of life informed by social and moral responsibility, raised awareness and strong and steady mind to strive for human excellence.



Following are some of the recent activities of Vivekananda Samiti:


Weekly activities
  • Weekly Vedanta class: Different Vedantic texts like Bhagwad Gita, Vivek Chudamani by Adi Shankaracharya, Isavasya Upanishad are studied under the competent guidance of Mrs. Archana T. This class is held every Wednesday 9-10:30 pm. Attendance is about 10-15.

  • Weekly Satsanga session: We commence by Vedic Chanting, singing Stotra, Meditation followed by study of Ramakrishna Vivekananda Vedanta literature. We discuss the teachings and philosophy of great saints and sages, understanding the relevance of Swami Vivekananda's message for the youth. Attendance varies from 10-15.


Activities of 150th birth anniversary of Swami Vivekananda
  • Vivekananda Youth Convention 2012 (Jan 14-15, 2012): After the success of the first youth convention in 2011, we organized VYC 2012 with even richer talks and fascinating competitions. This 2-day event was held in L-16. Besides talks on Swami Vivekananda's life and teachings, we touched on topics like "Karma Yoga and Fearlessness", "Youth Empowerment", "Setting goals in life", "Stress and Time Management" and the "Science and Art of Communication". The speakers included monks, practitioners, scholars and IAS and IPS officials, and faculty members and counselors from within the campus.

    Competitions included Written Quiz on Indian Culture, Group Discussion, Extempore and Essay Writing on "The Role of Youth in Nation Building." We had fairly good participation in all the competitions. Prizes and certificates worth over Rs.10,000/ were awarded. For more details, visit VYC 2012 Webpage: http://www.iitk.ac.in/vs/vyc_2012/index.php

    We acknowledge our DOSA for availing the funding of Rs. 96K for this event. Special thanks to our VYC team comprising of over 30 volunteers.


  • National Youth Day Celebration (12th Jan, 2012): We had couple of short talks by Prof. A.K. Ghosh and Prof. C. S. Upadhyay on the challenges and Opportunities before today's youth. Then we had an audience quiz on Swami Vivekananda followed by a movie show on Swami Vivekananda's life and message. Participation: 110

  • Spirit of India (13th August, 2011): The objective of the event was to take inspiration from our glorious past and motivate ourselves towards building a better and stronger India. We wanted to enlighten the campus community of the essential spirit of India- Unity in Diversity. Swami Vivekananda said that it is these values which bind this vast nation; it is the spirituality which is the life blood of this nation. Download the report (pdf)

  • Vivekananda Youth Convention 2011 (Jan 15-16, 2011): a 2 day celebration of Swami Vivekananda's birthday. 5 guest speakers gave inspiring talks. There were competitions like essay, debate and quiz. The budget of the program was over Rs. 70,000/. Attendance varied from 100-200 approximately. For more details, visit VYC 2011 Webpage: http://www.iitk.ac.in/vs/vyc_2011/index.html


Philanthropic activities
  • Medical Eye camp (19th Feb 2012): An Eye Camp was organized at Navasrijan Vidyalaya, Ratanpur near Nankari on Sunday 19th Feb., 2012 from 9:30 am to 1 pm. Dr. Anju Mishra with her team offered her voluntary service. About 75 patients came for examination. About 90% patients were found suffering from long or short-sightedness. They were provided with glasses at a subsidized price. Four Patients suffering from cataract were offered surgery and medication without any charges.

  • Medical health camp (12th Feb 2012): Vivekananda Samiti, IIT Kanpur organized a Medical health camp for about 375 under-privileged children and their parents in and around the campus. This program was conducted on Sunday, 12th Febrauary 2012 9:30 am to 1 pm with the help of Ramakrishna Mission Hospital, Kanpur at Shiksha Sopan Vidyalaya at Barasirohi village, near IIT Kanpur. This camp was arranged with the help of voluntary service of a team of four competent doctors: Dr. G.K. Tiwari, Dr. R.M. Gupta, Dr. Mohita Gulati and Dr. Sutapa Mandal, all of whom offer their services regularly at Ramakrishna Mission Hospital. This camp was inaugurated by Dr. H.C. Verma, President, Shiksha Sopan and Swami Tejomayananda, Secretary, Ramakrishna Mission Hospital. Among the other eminent guests included Dr. O.P. Mishra, Health Center, IITK who provided free medicines. Over 35 comitted volunteers from Vivekananda Samiti and Shiksha Sopan rendered their voluntary services to carry out the camp smoothly.

  • Collection and distribution of Old articles (First week of May 2011): Old clothes, unused medicine and other articles were collected from different hostels and distributed to the poor people in the campus, mostly the contract workers. About 12-15 students actively participated in this activity.

  • Medical health camp (Feb 2011) for 200 under privileged children at Shiksha Sopan, a NGO school in a nearby village. This program was jointly organized by Vivekananda Samiti, Ramakrishna Mission Hospital, Kanpur and Shiksha Sopan.


Lecture series
  • Lecture Series on Individual and Social Awakening by Swami Tyagarupananda (17th & 18th Mar, 2012): Swami Tyagarupananda is an eminent scholar and an excellent orator in the Ramakrishna Order. He is the Principal of the Ramakrishna Mission Vidyamandir, the residential college at Belur Math, Howrah, West Bengal, since 2005. He earned his PhD. degree in Economics from the Calcutta University in 1993. He shared his thoughts on "Enlightened Citizenship and the Indian Society" and "Karma Yoga - the doctrine of Swami Vivekananda revisited on his 150th Birth Anniversary." We had a gathering of around 60. For more details, visit the webpage.

  • Talk on Challenges of Modern Life and their Vedic Solutions (25th Feb, 2012): This talk was jointly organized by Health Centre and Vivekananda Samiti, IIT Kanpur. Swami Dharmanishthanandaji and Sri Ramesh Mishra ji from Divine Life Society shared their views on the Vedic way of life and emphasized on improving our daily routine and sparing some time for prayer, meditation and self analysis every day. Dr. O P Mishra took the lead role in organizing the event. Deputy Director, Prof. S C Srivastava inaugurated the function. Attendance was close to 90.

  • Talk on Ancient Indian Contribution by Dr. Priyadarshi (18th Feb, 2012): An eminent author and scholar, Dr. Premendra Priyadarshi shared his research work on "Gimpses of Classical Physics in Ancient India" and "India- the Origin of Worlds' Civilizations as evident from Genetic Studies". This talk gave enriching and exciting facts of our rich scientific and cultural heritage. It was attended by around 100 people. For more details, visit the webpage.

Book stalls
  • Sale and Exhibition of Ramakrishna Vivekananda Vedanta Literature: We organized the sale and exhibition of Ramakrishna Vivekananda Vedanta literature in association with Ramakrishna Mission Ashram, Kanpur during Antaragni, 2011. Books of around Rs. 20,000/ were sold through our book stall at the Media Center lawns and at different hostels. Some free books were also distributed. We are once again organizing the book stalls at different hostel premises from March 17-25, 2012.


Temple tours
  • Kanpur City Temple Tour (18th September, 2011): Kanpur city hosts some wonderful old temples of the times of Ramayana and Mahabharata and few modern temples, many of which are on the banks of Ganges. Vivekananda Samiti conducted a temple tour in and around Kanpur city. The tour includes temples at Shivrajpur, Bithoor and Kanpur city. In the afternoon, we have taken Bhog Prasad at Jagganath Temple on Bithoor road. We visited 2 temples in Shibrajpur, Brahmavarta Ghat, Sai Darbar at Bithur, 3-4 temples in Kanpur city. Tour ended with the visit of JK Temple in the city. Participation: 85

It was a wonderful experience to see young students studying and knowing about Swami Vivekananda.
